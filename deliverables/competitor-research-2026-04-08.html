Competitor Ecosystem Research

How the savings industry captures user lifetime value — and where the gaps are for Groupon

Executive Summary

Five things you need to know

  • Honey is hemorrhaging users — lost 8M Chrome users since the affiliate commission scandal broke. A class action lawsuit was filed in Jan 2026. The trust vacuum is real and unfilled.
  • RetailMeNot is declining — Ziff Davis reported an 18% revenue decline in the Tech & Shopping segment. Pure affiliate/SEO models are under structural pressure from AI and search shifts.
  • Safari iOS extensions are the new battleground — Honey (via PayPal), Rakuten, and Capital One Shopping all have Safari Web Extensions on iOS. Capital One Shopping is the #2 free Safari app. This is where mobile shopping capture happens.
  • Community = stickiness — Slickdeals achieves a 40% DAU/MAU ratio (social media-level) through user-generated deals and FOMO mechanics. Fetch Rewards hits 34% via receipt scanning gamification. Both dwarf typical shopping app engagement.
  • Groupon has unique advantages nobody else has — 30M+ app installs for extension bundling, Local + Coupons cross-sell that no pure-play competitor can match, and deep direct merchant relationships. The pieces exist; the ecosystem doesn't.

Market Timing

The Honey Crisis — Why NOW Is the Right Time

The savings industry is experiencing its biggest trust disruption in a decade. Honey — the category leader with 20M users — lost 8M users in 2025 after revelations that it was:

  • Stealing affiliate commissions from content creators by replacing their tracking cookies
  • Showing worse coupons to users to earn higher commissions from merchants
  • Prioritizing PayPal's revenue over user savings

The timeline tells the story:

-3M First 2 weeks
-4M By May 2025
-8M By end of 2025
Lawsuit Class action Jan 2026
The window is open: 8 million users are actively looking for an alternative they can trust. Coupert is capturing some (73% success rate, privacy-first), but no major brand has moved to fill the vacuum. Groupon has the brand recognition, merchant relationships, and existing extension (Genie) to claim this space — but the window won't stay open forever as Rakuten and Capital One Shopping accelerate.

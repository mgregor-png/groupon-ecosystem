## 1. TL;DR - what each adds beyond the April 2026 doc

- **Ibotta** is not a consumer app story any more. It is a B2B2C infrastructure play. The IPN (Ibotta Performance Network) now powers Walmart Cash, Dollar General, Family Dollar, Schnucks, Instacart (Nov 2024), and DoorDash (Q2 2025 full rollout). 64% of Q4 2025 revenue came from third-party publishers, 25% direct-to-consumer. [Data] Distribution, not app engagement, is the moat. [Opinion]
- **RetailMeNot** launched a 1% guaranteed cashback floor at 4,000+ retailers on Feb 25, 2026. Timing is defensive: Ziff Davis Tech & Shopping segment revenue fell 18% in Q4 2025 with a $25M YoY affiliate-commission drop blamed on declining high-intent search traffic. [Data] The 1% floor is a retention-of-affiliate-attribution play, not a loyalty reinvention. [Opinion]
- **Coupert** is a bootstrapped, 8-11 person operation (Delaware + Kowloon) with no disclosed VC funding and ~$1.2M revenue per one secondary source. The $1 withdrawal floor and 72.92% claimed auto-apply success rate are real differentiators vs Honey's 33%. [Data] Post-Honey-shutdown migration narrative not confirmed in sources - Trustpilot reviews mixed, some users alleging $600+ unpaid balances. [Data]
- **Starbucks** launched a redesigned tiered program (Green / Gold / Reserve) on March 10, 2026 under Niccol. 35.5M 90-day active members (Q1 FY26, all-time high). Rewards members = ~60% of US company-operated revenue FY25 ($13B+). Mobile order = 31% of transactions (Q1 FY25). Stored card value = $1.78B at end of FY24. Breakage revenue = $207M in 2024. [Data] The single most important habit driver is not stars - it is the **pre-loaded card + mobile order wallet lock-in**, which creates a sunk-cost + friction-free-reorder combination. [Opinion]

## 2. Ibotta - IPN and B2B2C distribution model

### The shift
The April 2026 doc covers Ibotta as a receipt-scan cashback app. That framing is increasingly wrong. Q4 2025 revenue mix: 64% third-party publishers, 25% D2C. [Data] Full-year 2025: $342.4M revenue, down 7% YoY. D2C revenue fell 25% to $139.9M. Third-party publishers rose 12% to $202.5M. [Data] IPN had 20.4M Q4 redeemers, up 19% YoY. [Data]

### Partner list (confirmed)
- **Walmart** - IPN powers the Walmart Cash program across Walmart.com and the Walmart app [Data]
- **Dollar General** - live partnership with DG app offers [Data]
- **Family Dollar** - confirmed on Ibotta's IPN marketing [Data]
- **Schnucks** - regional grocery, confirmed [Data]
- **Instacart** - launched Nov 2024 [Data]
- **DoorDash** - multi-year partnership, announced Jan 2025, majority of users live Q2 2025 [Data]
- Kroger is named in partner lists but confirmation of active integration is weaker. [Assumption]

### Take-rate economics
Ibotta does not publicly disclose the revenue-split ratio with publishers. [Data gap] What is disclosed:
- Redemption revenue per redemption = $0.83 in Q4 2025, down 5% YoY from $0.87. [Data]
- Redemptions per redeemer fell from 5.5 to 4.6 YoY. [Data]
- Model is fee-per-redemption paid by CPG brand to Ibotta, split with publisher. [Data]
- Implication: as the redeemer base broadens via IPN partners, intensity-per-user drops. This is the classic B2B2C dilution risk. [Opinion]

### Public company status
IPO'd April 2024 (NYSE: IBTA). FY2024 revenue $368M (reported in prior April 2026 doc). FY2025: $342.4M, net income $3.6M (1% margin), Adjusted EBITDA $62.9M (18% margin). [Data] 2025 was down year due to D2C decline. Guidance for H1 2026 implied further softness. [Data]

### Key mechanic Groupon could learn
**The B2B2C distribution pattern**. Ibotta solved the "stop begging for app installs" problem by embedding offer logic inside partners' owned wallets (Walmart Cash, DoorDash savings). Groupon could plug cashback into partner checkout flows rather than forcing users through the Groupon app. [Opinion]

### Key weakness
**Revenue-per-user decline**. More redeemers, fewer redemptions each, lower $ per redemption. The network is growing but the engine is coughing. Over-exposure to grocery CPG (which is low-margin and commoditising) is the structural issue. [Opinion]

## 3. RetailMeNot - the 1% guaranteed floor move

### The floor itself
- Announced Feb 25, 2026. [Data]
- 1% minimum cashback, stackable on top of promo codes. [Data]
- 4,000+ participating retailers. [Data]
- Explicitly positioned as "industry benchmark" and "always-on savings floor". [Data]

### Why now - the economic rationale
Ziff Davis Q4 2025 (Feb 2026 earnings):
- Technology & Shopping segment revenue = $108.9M, down 18% YoY from $132.9M. [Data]
- Adjusted EBITDA for the segment = $47.2M, down 19.1%. [Data]
- Management attributed this to a **$25M YoY drop in affiliate commissions**, driven by declining high-intent search traffic. [Data]
- Stock fell 11-13% on the Q4 miss. [Data]
- Management guided Tech & Shopping to **double-digit decline H1 2026**, improving to mid-single-digit decline full-year. [Data]

**Interpretation:** The 1% floor is not a loyalty innovation. It is a **survival move** for an affiliate model under existential pressure from AI-search displacing product queries. RetailMeNot is buying back attribution by paying consumers directly. [Opinion]

### Portfolio consolidation
The "RetailMeNot Group" (unveiled Jan 2026 at 20th anniversary) consolidates: RetailMeNot, Offers.com, BlackFriday.com, TechBargains, Deals of America. [Data] R BrandWorks is the in-house influencer creative studio. [Data]

### 100M+ social reach
- Reaches 100M+ consumers across TikTok, Instagram, and other platforms (per Jan 2026 press release). [Data]
- No specific per-platform follower counts disclosed in searched sources. [Data gap]
- 2025 claim: $1.7B in facilitated retailer sales, $100M+ in cashback paid to consumers. [Data]

### Key mechanic Groupon could learn
**Guaranteed floor as a trust primitive**. "You will always get at least 1% back" turns a cashback site into a rational default. It removes shopping around from the consumer's decision tree. If Groupon did "at minimum X% back guaranteed on every deal" it would reframe the value prop from "find-a-deal" to "always-saving". [Opinion]

### Key weakness
**The floor doesn't fix the input problem**. RetailMeNot's traffic is eroding because Google/LLMs are intercepting the high-intent shopping query. 1% cashback doesn't draw new users if users never arrive. This is a rearguard action, not a growth engine. [Opinion]

## 4. Coupert - post-Honey migration and $1 hook

### The $1 withdrawal mechanic
- $1 minimum via Visa/Mastercard in the US (2% + $0.50 processing fee applies). [Data]
- $2 first-time PayPal/bank threshold for new users. $10 for subsequent PayPal/bank withdrawals. [Data]
- Cashback confirmed in 7-15 days (vs Honey's 60-90 days). [Data]

### Psychological effect
The $1 floor breaks the traditional cashback pattern of "you have to spend a lot to ever withdraw". Honey required 1,000 points (~$10) in gift-card form only. Coupert's $1 means a user can validate the system works on their very first small purchase. That is an onboarding trust-primitive, not a cashback mechanic. [Opinion]

### Dual currency (cash + Gold Coins)
- Cash: direct dollar cashback earned per purchase [Data]
- Gold Coins: separate rewards-program currency, 1,000 Gold = $10 gift card [Data]
- Value ratio: 1 Gold ~ 1 cent [Data]
- Split purpose: cash for monetary reward, Gold for engagement/gamification actions (referrals, daily check-ins implied). [Assumption - structure described but mechanic gaps]

### The 72.92% success rate claim
- Methodology: side-by-side test of 100 real shopping scenarios [Data]
- Coupert's ranking engine prioritises codes with real-time crowdsourced success signals [Data]
- Honey applied codes in 33.33% of same scenarios [Data]
- Source: Coupert's own research blog. **Self-reported, not independently audited.** [Data - with integrity caveat]

### Post-Honey migration evidence
- Searched sources did NOT confirm a Honey shutdown. [Data gap - the original research assumption may be incorrect, or Honey's PayPal-era degradation is being framed as "shutdown"]
- Trustpilot: mixed reviews. Positive users cite consistent savings. Negative users (including a $600+ unpaid complaint) cite nonresponsive support. [Data]
- Specific review growth rate / velocity not available in searched sources. [Data gap]

### Company profile
- Founded 2017-2018. [Data - slight source variance]
- Team size: 8-11 employees. [Data]
- HQ: Dover, DE + Kowloon, HK. [Data]
- Funding: None disclosed. Bootstrapped. [Data]
- Revenue: ~$1.2M per one secondary source (Latka). [Estimate - single source]
- Founders: not disclosed in public sources. [Data gap]

### Key mechanic Groupon could learn
**The $1 floor as an activation trigger.** Let the user cash out at any amount with a small fee. Converts the cashback from a distant future reward into an immediate usable reward. Groupon credit has exactly this problem - it accumulates and expires. If Groupon allowed cashback-to-PayPal at $1 with a processing fee, users would lose the "abandoned balance" trust problem. [Opinion]

### Key weakness
**Scale and trust ceiling.** An 8-11 person bootstrapped team cannot build the merchant direct-deals that Rakuten/Honey enjoy. Revenue is small. Trustpilot complaints about unpaid balances suggest the operational side is under-resourced. Not a category leader - a category parasite. [Opinion]

## 5. Starbucks - the pure-loyalty benchmark

*This is the deepest section. Starbucks is the only pure-loyalty benchmark in the scope because Marriott Bonvoy was deliberately excluded (wrong frequency cadence per intake).*

### 5.1 Stars mechanic (post-March 10, 2026)

**Earning rate:**
- Green tier: 1 star per $1 spent [Data]
- Gold tier (500+ stars earned in prior year): 1.2 stars per $1 [Data]
- Reserve tier (2,500+ stars earned in prior year): 1.7 stars per $1 [Data]
- Previous program gave 2 stars per $1 on reloaded Starbucks Card [Data] - this is the devaluation trigger that caused backlash [Data]

**Star redemption values:**
- 25 stars = free customization (e.g. extra espresso shot, dairy alternative) [Data]
- 100 stars = free brewed coffee, hot tea, or bakery item [Data]
- 200 stars = free handcrafted drink (any size, any customization) - capped at $10 in dollar value [Data]
- 400 stars = free merchandise [Data]
- At Green tier's 1 star / $1 rate, a 200-star handcrafted-drink reward requires $200 of spend. Previously $100 under the 2-star rate. [Data - this is the math behind the backlash]

### 5.2 Tiering

**Three tiers, launched March 10, 2026:**
- **Green**: default. 1 star / $1. Free birthday reward. Stars can be prevented from expiring via monthly activity. [Data]
- **Gold**: 500 stars earned in prior year. 1.2 stars / $1. 4 additional Double Star Days per year. Stars never expire. Birthday reward usable 7 days. [Data]
- **Reserve**: 2,500 stars earned in prior year. 1.7 stars / $1. 6 additional Double Star Days per year. Stars never expire. [Data]

**Tier maintenance:** Tier requalification is based on stars earned in the *prior calendar year*. Stars for tier qualification are separate from stars for redemption. [Data]

**Backlash signals:**
- "I Am So Done" - Takeout article [Data]
- PYMNTS framed this as "retail shift away from points-based loyalty" [Data]
- Reddit, X, Instagram longtime members complaining about effective devaluation [Data]
- TikTok critique hit 500k+ views, 3,400 replies in a day [Data]
- Commenter: held Gold since 2013, "required to restart as Green-tier" [Data]

### 5.3 Mobile order + pre-load

**The real money mechanic** (and Opinion: the core of Starbucks' habit engine, not the stars):

- 31% of US company-operated store transactions came through the mobile app at end of FY24. [Data] Up from 27% prior year, 25% two years before. [Data]
- Stored card value = $1.872B as of Q2 FY24. $1.78B at end of FY24. [Data]
- $207M in breakage revenue (unredeemed balances) in FY24. [Data] - up from $141M in FY19. [Data]
- This balance is "an interest-free line of credit equal to ~4% of total liabilities" [Estimate - analyst framing]
- Bonus Stars on Starbucks Card reloads remains a headline universal benefit post-March 2026 redesign. [Data]

**Why pre-load drives habit:**
Wallet lock-in. Once a user has $25+ sitting in the Starbucks app, the mental cost of "not using it" dominates the cost of choosing Starbucks over alternatives. This is not rewards - it is sunk-cost psychology formalised. [Opinion]

### 5.4 Streaks, challenges, gamification

- **Double Star Days**: disappeared for ~year after Sept 2024. Returned Sept 19-20, 2025. [Data] "Star Days event" announced April 6-27, 2026 with weekly offers. [Data]
- **Triple Star Tuesdays** ran through Sept 2024, then discontinued. [Data]
- **Personalised challenges**: "buy 3 breakfast items this week, earn X bonus stars". Cadence: every few weeks. [Data]
- **Free Mod Mondays**: new universal benefit post March 2026 - free customization mod on Mondays. [Data]
- **Bonus Stars on reload**: recurring, incentivises pre-load action. [Data]
- **Starbucks for Life** sweepstakes: returns 2025 with "nearly 10 million prizes" including viral Bearista Cold Cup. [Data]

### 5.5 Starbucks Odyssey / Web3 experiment

- Launched closed beta Sept 2022. [Data]
- **Shut down March 31, 2024**. [Data]
- Starbucks: "Odyssey Beta must come to an end to prepare for what comes next" [Data] - no explicit replacement announced. [Data]
- Stamps (NFTs) transferred to Nifty Gateway for continued trading. [Data]
- **Lesson**: Web3 added complexity without frequency benefit. The base Rewards program already had 30M+ members - Odyssey was layered on top for power users and failed to ladder into the mass program. [Opinion]

### 5.6 Post-Niccol changes (CEO since Sept 2024)

**Changes under Niccol relevant to loyalty:**
- Eliminated the 25-star bonus for bringing reusable cups (June 24, 2025), replaced with "double stars on entire order" [Data]
- Reinstated free refills and condiment bars in US cafes (Jan 2025) [Data]
- March 10, 2026 Rewards redesign (the tier overhaul) [Data]
- Strategy framing: "boost profits, dial back deals to protect margins" [Data]

### 5.7 Member scale and economics

- **35.5M 90-day active US members** - Q1 FY26, all-time high. [Data]
- 34.3M earlier in 2025. [Data] (Membership still growing despite backlash.)
- Rewards = **59-60% of US company-operated revenue FY25** - $13B+ spend. [Data]
- Q2 2025: 57% of US transactions. [Data]
- DAU/MAU ratio: not publicly disclosed. [Data gap]
- Frequency per rewards member vs non-member: not publicly disclosed. [Data gap]

### 5.8 Habit drivers ranked (for Groupon)

1. **Stored value + mobile order combo** - biggest habit lever. Forces pre-commitment, removes friction at point of purchase. [Opinion]
2. **Bonus stars on reload** - puts fuel in the stored-value engine. [Opinion]
3. **Free Mod Mondays / universal weekday tie** - day-of-week anchor. [Opinion]
4. **Bonus star challenges** - specific tasks on short time horizons (buy X this week for Y bonus). [Opinion]
5. **Tier benefits / status** - pulls the top ~20% of spenders into higher-spend behaviour. [Opinion]
6. **Base star earn** - the cashback baseline. Lowest leverage, because 1-1.7 stars/$ at $0.01/star is a ~1-1.7% effective return. [Opinion]

### 5.9 What Groupon can borrow (and what it can't)

**Borrowable:**
- **Weekly anchor** (equivalent of "Free Mod Mondays") - Groupon could commit to one weekday with a guaranteed rate uplift. Creates a reason to open the app on a specific day. [Opinion]
- **Challenge engine** - personalised time-bound missions ("use 3 deals this week, 10% extra back"). Groupon has the deal catalogue to support this. [Opinion]
- **Tier structure** - identify top ~5% of spenders, give them named status, let them extract above-average rate. Retention of the top cohort is the highest-ROI investment in any loyalty system. [Opinion]
- **The trust floor** (RetailMeNot-style 1% floor cross-applied here) + **cashout at $1** (Coupert-style) - together these would turn Groupon credit into real money in the user's perception. [Opinion]

**NOT borrowable - the Starbucks secret sauce that doesn't cross over:**
- **Pre-loaded stored value**: Starbucks works because coffee is a *consumable* with deterministic price, daily frequency, near-zero stockout risk. Users can credibly pre-pay $50 and know they will redeem within 30 days. Groupon purchase is *episodic* and unpredictable in both timing and amount. Pre-loading into a Groupon wallet would feel like lending Groupon money without a consumption guarantee. The stored-value habit loop does not port to general e-commerce or local deals. [Opinion]
- **The physical store ritual**: Starbucks habit is partially spatial (you pass the store on your commute). Online cashback has no spatial ritual. [Opinion]
- **Daily frequency**: Starbucks benchmark is daily-to-weekly. Cashback/coupons benchmark is weekly-to-monthly. You cannot manufacture daily habit out of a monthly-cadence behaviour - you can only push weekly to weekly-twice. [Opinion]

## 6. Cross-competitor pattern - what Tier 2 players share that Tier 1 doesn't

- **All three Tier 2 players (Ibotta, RetailMeNot, Coupert) are under structural pressure.**
  - Ibotta: D2C down 25%, revenue per redemption declining [Data]
  - RetailMeNot parent: segment revenue down 18%, affiliate commissions down $25M YoY, double-digit decline guided for H1 2026 [Data]
  - Coupert: bootstrapped with Trustpilot payment complaints, no disclosed scale [Data]
- **All three are pivoting toward "floor" mechanics:**
  - Ibotta → B2B2C distribution to ensure offers reach users at scale
  - RetailMeNot → guaranteed 1% cashback floor
  - Coupert → $1 withdrawal floor
- **Pattern for Groupon:** Tier 2 is signalling that the cashback-extension category is commoditising. The floor mechanic (minimum guaranteed value) is the emerging competitive move because differentiation on search/UX is being eroded by AI-assistant intermediation. [Opinion]
- **Tier 1 (Rakuten, Cap One, Honey pre-shutdown) don't need floors - they compete on merchant exclusives and card-linked offers.** Tier 2 is trying to substitute "rate certainty" for "merchant depth". [Opinion]
- **None of the three Tier 2 players has a habit engine comparable to Starbucks.** Ibotta's receipt-scan is chore-like, RetailMeNot is session-triggered by shopping intent, Coupert is passive auto-apply. All three lack a weekly anchor, a challenge engine, or a stored-value lock-in. [Opinion]

## 8. Red flags / Yellow flags

### Red flags
- **RetailMeNot's 1% floor is a rearguard move, not innovation.** If Ziff Davis Tech & Shopping keeps sliding as guided (double-digit decline H1 2026), the entire RetailMeNot Group could be under strategic review within 12 months. Don't model them as a stable competitor. [Opinion]
- **Ibotta revenue-per-redemption declining** while redeemer count grows. The network is diluting. If this continues, IPN economics could crack at the publisher-split level. [Opinion]
- **Coupert Trustpilot complaints about unpaid balances ($600+ alleged).** With 8-11 staff and no funding, operational risk is real. The 72.92% success rate claim is self-reported and unaudited. Treat as marketing, not data. [Opinion]

### Yellow flags
- **Starbucks Rewards redesign backlash may or may not bite.** 35.5M active members is still growing. But social-media sentiment suggests the high-engagement cohort (former Gold-holders of 10+ years) feel devalued. This matters because that cohort drives disproportionate frequency. Watch Q2 FY26 (July 2026) membership and transaction data. [Opinion]
- **"Honey shutdown" narrative** in the intake may be inaccurate. Searched sources do not confirm a Honey shutdown. Coupert's own comparison blogs benchmark against Honey as a live competitor. Re-verify this assumption before depending on it. [Data - flagging for Martin]
- **Starbucks Odyssey is a cautionary tale on over-engineering loyalty.** Don't add a blockchain/NFT/complex layer on top of a working program. The base program had the engagement - Odyssey didn't ladder into it. [Opinion]
- **Starbucks date context**: Much of the 2026 reporting is from Jan-March 2026, so "Q1 FY26" = roughly Dec 2025-March 2026. [Data - calendaring note]

## 1. RetailMeNot (Ziff Davis / J2 Global)

**The closest comp to Groupon Coupons — same affiliate model, same SEO dependency.**

### Business Model
- **Primary:** Affiliate commissions from coupon codes and deal links (same as Groupon Coupons)
- **Secondary:** Cash back (guaranteed 1% across 4,000+ merchants, up to 20% at select retailers)
- **Tertiary:** Sponsored placements and brand advertising via R BrandWorks
- Acquired by Ziff Davis (J2 Global) in 2020 for **$420M**
- Now unified as "RetailMeNot Group" — consolidating RetailMeNot, Offers.com, BlackFriday.com, TechBargains, Deals of America, and R BrandWorks

### Platforms
| Platform | Available? | Details |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Website | Yes | ~18M monthly visits (SimilarWeb, May 2025) |
| iOS App | Yes | "RetailMeNot: Cash Back & Codes" on App Store |
| Android App | Yes | Available on Google Play |
| Chrome Extension | Yes | ~600K users, 4.51 rating (638 reviews) |
| Firefox Extension | Yes | Available |
| Safari Extension (Desktop) | Yes | Available via Mac App Store |
| Safari Web Extension (iOS) | Yes | Mobile Safari extension available |

### Key Metrics
- **Monthly web visits:** ~18M (down from peak; 45.67% direct, 43.77% organic search)
- **Cash back paid out:** $100M+ total to members
- **Retailer sales facilitated:** $1.7B through cash back platform
- **Chrome extension users:** ~600K
- **Revenue (parent segment):** Ziff Davis Tech & Shopping segment saw **18% revenue decline** in Q4, linked to weaker search traffic and affiliate commissions

### Ecosystem Mechanics
- Cash back balance payable via PayPal, Venmo, or debit card
- AI-powered shopping companion (beta) for deal curation
- Year-round exclusive savings events (Cash Back Day)
- Stacking: cash back works on top of coupon codes

### What Groupon Could Learn
- **Cash back as affiliate hedge:** RetailMeNot's guaranteed 1% cash back across 4K merchants is a direct response to declining affiliate commissions. This shifts from pure traffic-driven affiliate revenue to relationship-based marketing spend. Groupon should watch whether this stabilizes their economics.
- **Brand consolidation:** Unifying 6 shopping brands under one roof (RetailMeNot Group) to cross-sell audiences — Groupon has similar untapped potential between Coupons, Local, and Genie.
- **Warning sign:** 18% revenue decline shows how vulnerable pure affiliate/SEO models are to search traffic shifts and AI disruption.

## 2. Honey (PayPal Honey)

**The cautionary tale — $4B acquisition now hemorrhaging users after trust scandal.**

### Business Model
- **Primary:** Affiliate commissions (1-15% per transaction) via last-click attribution
- **Secondary:** Data insights for PayPal's commerce platform
- **Tertiary:** Honey Gold rewards (points redeemable for gift cards, 1,000 points ~ $10)
- Acquired by PayPal in January 2020 for **~$4B**

### Platforms
| Platform | Available? | Details |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Website | Yes | joinhoney.com |
| iOS App | Yes | "PayPal Honey: Coupons, Rewards" |
| Android App | Yes | Available on Google Play |
| Chrome Extension | Yes | Was 20M users at peak, now ~13M (Dec 2025) |
| Firefox Extension | Yes | Available |
| Safari Extension (Desktop) | Yes | Available |
| Safari Web Extension (iOS) | **Yes — bundled with PayPal app** | Auto-installed when PayPal app is installed; requires user activation in Safari settings |

### Key Metrics
- **Peak Chrome users:** ~20M (pre-controversy)
- **Current Chrome users:** ~13M (Dec 2025) — lost ~8M users since peak
- **User loss timeline:** 3M lost in first 2 weeks of scandal; 4M by May 2025; 8M by end of 2025
- **Acquisition price:** ~$4B (widely called a massive overpay)
- **Average user savings:** $126/year (Honey's claim)
- **Class action lawsuit:** Filed early Jan 2026 (affiliate commission theft allegations)

### Safari iOS Extension — How Activation Works
This is the most relevant competitive intelligence for Genie:
1. User installs the PayPal app from App Store
2. The Honey Safari Web Extension is **automatically bundled** — no separate download needed
3. Extension appears in Safari Settings > Extensions but is **not activated by default**
4. User must go to Settings > Safari > Extensions > PayPal Honey > toggle "Allow Extension"
5. Available in US, CA, AU, UK, DE, FR, NL, IT, ES, IN (iOS 15+)
6. Significant user complaints about inability to fully remove the extension

### Ecosystem Mechanics
- Honey Gold points system (earn on purchases, redeem for gift cards)
- Automatic coupon testing at checkout
- Price history tracking and price drop alerts
- Droplist (wishlist with price monitoring)
- PayPal integration for seamless payments

### What Groupon Could Learn
- **Bundling power (and its limits):** Auto-installing a Safari extension via the PayPal app is brilliant distribution. But the activation rate appears low because users must manually enable it in Settings. Genie faces the exact same challenge.
- **Trust is everything:** Honey's scandal (allegedly stealing affiliate commissions from creators, showing worse coupons to earn higher commissions) destroyed user trust. Any extension that intercepts the checkout flow must be transparently beneficial to the user.
- **Coupon success rate matters:** Honey's 33% coupon success rate (per Coupert's testing) is a vulnerability. If Groupon can deliver higher hit rates, that's a real differentiator.

## 3. Rakuten (Rakuten Rewards, formerly Ebates)

**The gold standard of cashback — 17M US members, $3.7B paid out.**

### Business Model
- **Primary:** Affiliate commissions shared with users as cash back (typically keeps 30-50% of commission)
- **Secondary:** Sponsored placements and featured merchant positions
- **Tertiary:** Data and insights for partner merchants
- US cashback business (Rakuten Rewards) is a subsidiary of Rakuten Group (Tokyo)
- Rakuten Group FY2024 consolidated revenue: **¥2.3T (~$15.5B)**, up 10% YoY

### Platforms
| Platform | Available? | Details |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Website | Yes | rakuten.com — 3,500+ partner stores |
| iOS App | Yes | "Rakuten: Cash Back & Deals" |
| Android App | Yes | Available |
| Chrome Extension | Yes | "Rakuten: Cash Back & Coupons" |
| Firefox Extension | Yes | Available |
| Safari Extension (Desktop) | Yes | Available via Mac App Store |
| Safari Web Extension (iOS) | **Yes** | Mobile Safari extension activates cash back directly on store sites |

### Key Metrics
- **US members:** 17M+
- **Total cash back paid (US):** $3.7B+ since 1997
- **Q2 2024 GMS:** ~$2.78B (US division)
- **Q2 2024 operating income:** $10M (US division, down from $12M YoY)
- **Partner stores:** 3,500+
- **Cash back rates:** 1-15% depending on merchant
- **Rakuten Group revenue:** $15.5B (FY2024), $16.7B (2025 annualized)

### Safari iOS Extension — How It Works
- Requires downloading the Rakuten app from App Store
- Enable extension in Safari Settings > Extensions > Rakuten
- Extension activates cash back directly on store sites in mobile Safari
- Also finds and applies coupon codes
- Price comparison alerts when another store has better price/cash back

### Ecosystem Mechanics
- **Cash back balance as retention moat:** Users accumulate cash back that pays out quarterly (via check or PayPal). This creates a "balance" that keeps users coming back.
- **Big Fat Check:** Quarterly payout events marketed as "Big Fat Check" — creates anticipation
- **In-store cash back:** Link credit/debit card for offline earning
- **Referral program:** $30 per referred friend
- **Flash deals and bonus events:** Double/triple cash back promotions

### What Groupon Could Learn
- **Cash back balance = retention:** The accumulated balance is a powerful flywheel. Users check back because they have money waiting. Groupon has no equivalent retention mechanic in Coupons.
- **Quarterly payout cadence:** Paying out quarterly (not instantly) keeps users engaged longer and creates marketing moments.
- **The 3,500-store network** is both a strength and a vulnerability — Groupon's merchant relationships through Local could be a unique angle if combined with coupons.
- **iOS Safari extension:** Rakuten has already solved the mobile Safari extension challenge. Their implementation is a direct reference for Genie.

## 4. Capital One Shopping (formerly Wikibuy)

**The banking app trojan horse — 10M+ users via Capital One's 100M+ customer base.**

### Business Model
- **Primary:** Affiliate commissions and merchant partnerships
- **Secondary:** Shopping Rewards (proprietary points redeemable for gift cards from 50+ retailers)
- **Tertiary:** Data enrichment for Capital One's financial products
- Originally Wikibuy (acquired by Capital One in 2018)
- **Does NOT require a Capital One account** — open to everyone

### Platforms
| Platform | Available? | Details |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Website | Yes | capitaloneshopping.com |
| iOS App | Yes | 4.9 stars, 960K+ reviews |
| Android App | Yes | 4.8 stars, 64K+ reviews |
| Chrome Extension | Yes | 6M+ customers |
| Firefox Extension | Yes | Available |
| Safari Extension (Desktop) | Yes | **#2 Top Free Safari App** (Jul 2025) |
| Safari Web Extension (iOS) | **Yes** | Bundled with iOS app, requires manual activation |

### Key Metrics
- **Total users:** 10M+ globally
- **Chrome extension users:** 6M+
- **Customer savings:** $160M+ in one year
- **App Store rating:** 4.9/5 (960K+ reviews) — best in class
- **Safari ranking:** #2 Top Free Apps in Safari App Store

### Safari iOS Extension — Activation Flow
1. Download "Capital One Shopping" app from App Store
2. Go to iPhone Settings > Safari > Extensions
3. Find "Capital One Shopping" and toggle "Allow Extension"
4. Set website permissions (all sites recommended)
5. Create or log in to account, verify email
6. Extension works independently from Capital One banking products

### Ecosystem Mechanics
- **Price comparison:** Automatically checks if a lower price exists at other retailers
- **Coupon auto-apply:** Tests codes at checkout
- **Shopping Rewards:** Earn points at participating stores, redeem for gift cards
- **Price drop alerts:** Watchlist items with notifications
- **Universal credit:** Works across Amazon, Walmart, and thousands of stores
- **No financial product tie-in required** — this is key for adoption

### What Groupon Could Learn
- **Distribution via parent app:** Capital One has 100M+ customers. Even a small conversion rate to Shopping users yields massive scale. Groupon has 30M+ app installs — same principle applies for Genie.
- **Safari #2 ranking:** They've cracked Safari distribution. Worth studying their ASO and onboarding flow.
- **App Store ratings:** 4.9 stars across 960K reviews is extraordinary. Their UX is a benchmark.
- **Independence from parent:** Making the service free for non-Capital One customers dramatically expanded TAM. Genie should not gate behind Groupon account requirements.

## 5. Slickdeals

**The community flywheel — 12M MAU with 40% converting to daily actives.**

### Business Model
- **Primary:** Affiliate marketing commissions (backbone of revenue)
- **Secondary:** Sponsored placements / featured deal positions
- **Tertiary:** Display advertising (Google Ad Manager + private deals)
- **Quaternary:** Data analytics services for brands (conversion behavior, engagement metrics)
- Estimated annual revenue: **$35-50M** (2025)
- ~200-220 employees

### Platforms
| Platform | Available? | Details |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Website | Yes | 25M monthly visitors |
| iOS App | Yes | Redesigned March 2025 |
| Android App | Yes | Available |
| Browser Extension | Yes | Chrome, Firefox |
| Safari Extension | Limited | Not a primary focus |
| Community Forum | Yes | Core of the product |

### Key Metrics
- **Monthly active users:** 12M+ (up from 10M in 2018)
- **Monthly visitors:** 25M
- **DAU/MAU ratio:** ~40% (extremely high for shopping category)
- **Weekly active mobile shoppers:** 600K+
- **Revenue:** $35-50M estimated
- **Deal postings:** Up 65% (recent period)

### Ecosystem Mechanics
- **Community-curated deals:** Users submit, vote, and discuss deals — creates trust and FOMO
- **Deal Alerts:** Customizable notifications for specific products/categories
- **Frontpage algorithm:** Community voting surfaces best deals (Reddit-like)
- **Reputation system:** Power users earn status, driving repeat engagement
- **Forums and discussion:** Active conversations around deals create stickiness beyond transactions
- **AI-powered deal discovery:** Implemented via Amazon SageMaker (re:Invent 2025 case study)

### What Groupon Could Learn
- **Community = moat:** Slickdeals' 40% DAU/MAU ratio is driven by user-generated content and community dynamics, not by cashback balances. This is fundamentally different from every other competitor and much harder to replicate.
- **FOMO mechanics:** Deals expire, popular items sell out, community excitement creates urgency that drives daily checking behavior.
- **Power users as content engine:** A small base of prolific deal-posters creates value for millions of lurkers. Groupon could explore user-submitted coupon codes or deal tips.
- **Revenue ceiling:** $35-50M revenue with 12M MAU suggests lower per-user monetization than cashback models. Community engagement does not directly correlate with affiliate conversion.

- **Primary:** Affiliate commissions from merchant partnerships (70K+ stores)
- **Secondary:** Cashback rewards shared with users (paid in real money via PayPal/bank transfer, not points)
- Independent company (not owned by a tech giant)

### Platforms

|----------|-----------|---------|

| iOS App | Yes | Available |
| Android App | Yes | Available |
| Chrome Extension | Yes | Primary distribution channel |
| Firefox Extension | Yes | Available |
| Edge Extension | Yes | Available |
| Safari Extension | Yes | Available |

### Key Metrics
- **Total users:** 8M+
- **Trustpilot rating:** 4.8/5 (10K+ reviews)
- **Coupon success rate:** 72.92% (vs. Honey's ~33% in auto-testing)
- **Average user savings:** $600/year
- **Supported stores:** 70,000+

### Ecosystem Mechanics
- **Real cash, not points:** Cashback paid in actual money (PayPal or bank transfer), not gift cards
- **Confirmation speed:** 7-15 days for cashback confirmation
- **Privacy-first positioning:** Does not collect PII, only anonymous functional data
- **No data selling:** Explicitly states they do not share data with third-party advertisers
- **Auto-apply at checkout:** Prioritizes high-success-rate coupons first

### What Groupon Could Learn
- **Post-Honey opportunity:** Coupert is directly benefiting from Honey's trust crisis. Their 73% coupon success rate vs Honey's 33% is a massive differentiator they actively market.
- **Real cash > points:** Users respond better to actual money than points systems. If Groupon launches cashback, consider direct cash payouts.
- **Privacy as competitive advantage:** In a post-Honey world, explicit privacy commitments are a differentiator.
- **70K stores:** Having 2x Rakuten's store count matters for coverage, even if individual partnerships are less deep.

## 7. Fetch Rewards

**The engagement monster — 34% DAU/MAU, approaching social media-level stickiness.**

### Business Model
- **Primary:** Brand partnerships (CPG companies, restaurants, retailers pay for visibility and access to shopper data)
- **Secondary:** Targeted offer campaigns for brands
- **Tertiary:** Consumer data insights and analytics
- **Not traditional affiliate** — Fetch is a data/advertising platform that uses rewards as the user acquisition mechanism
- **Revenue run rate:** $500M ARR (Q4 2024), 65% YoY growth
- **Valuation:** $2.5B (based on $583M total funding)

### Platforms
| Platform | Available? | Details |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| iOS App | Yes | Primary platform |
| Android App | Yes | Primary platform |
| Website | Yes | Minimal — app-first |
| Browser Extension | Yes | Secondary to app |
| Safari Extension | No | Not a focus |

### Key Metrics
- **Active users:** 12.5M+
- **Daily active users:** 5M+ (as of Aug 2022 milestone)
- **DAU/MAU ratio:** 34-35% — highest among top 50 shopping apps
- **Revenue run rate:** $500M ARR (Q4 2024)
- **YoY revenue growth:** 65%
- **Total rewards paid:** $1B+
- **Valuation:** $2.5B
- **Total funding:** $583M (including $110M debt, Sep 2025)
- **Comparison:** Starbucks app has ~15% DAU/MAU; Fetch's 34% puts it in the 54th percentile among **social** apps

### Ecosystem Mechanics
- **Receipt scanning:** Core mechanic — scan any receipt from any store/restaurant to earn points
- **Zero-friction earning:** No need to activate offers before purchase or shop through specific links
- **Gamification:** In-app gaming feature delivered extra $25M in rewards
- **Brand-specific offers:** Special point bonuses for buying specific products
- **Gift card redemption:** Points redeemable for gift cards, merchandise, charitable donations
- **Snap & earn simplicity:** The act of scanning a receipt takes 5 seconds and immediately shows points earned — dopamine hit

### What Groupon Could Learn
- **Post-purchase engagement > pre-purchase intent:** Fetch flipped the model. Instead of needing users before they shop, Fetch captures them after. This dramatically increases addressable moments.
- **Receipt scanning as data goldmine:** Every receipt tells Fetch what users buy, where, and how often. This data is what brands pay for. Groupon has transaction data but doesn't monetize it this way.
- **Gamification drives daily opens:** The 34% DAU/MAU ratio is driven by games, streaks, and the dopamine of scanning receipts. Groupon's engagement is purely transactional.
- **$500M ARR on $2.5B valuation** — this is a 5x revenue multiple, indicating investors see Fetch as a high-growth data/advertising business, not a coupons business.

## Strategic Implications for Groupon

### The Market Is Shifting
1. **Pure affiliate/SEO is declining** — RetailMeNot's 18% revenue drop and Honey's user exodus show the old model is under pressure
2. **Cashback is table stakes** — Every major player now offers it. Groupon Coupons does not. This is a gap.
3. **Extensions are the battleground** — Safari Web Extensions on iOS are the new frontier. Honey, Rakuten, and Capital One Shopping all have them. Genie needs to be here.
4. **Trust is a differentiator** — Honey's scandal created a vacuum. Coupert and Rakuten are filling it. There's an opening for a trusted brand.

### Groupon's Unique Advantages
1. **Existing app install base** (30M+) — same bundling opportunity as PayPal/Honey and Capital One
2. **Local + Coupons cross-sell** — no competitor has both digital coupons AND local experiences
3. **Merchant relationships** — deep direct merchant relationships that pure affiliate players lack
4. **Genie browser extension** — already in market, needs Safari Web Extension + cashback

### Recommended Areas to Investigate Further
1. **Cashback economics:** Model the margin impact of sharing affiliate commissions as cashback (Rakuten keeps 30-50%)
2. **Safari Web Extension activation rates:** What % of PayPal app users actually enable Honey in Safari? This is the key question for Genie's iOS strategy
3. **Fetch's data model:** Could Groupon offer brands similar post-purchase data insights from its transaction data?
4. **Community mechanics:** Could Groupon add user-contributed coupon codes or deal discussions to increase engagement?

Alternative Monetisation — Brainstorm Ideas

For discussion: Martin + Becky, 2026-03-26 Lens: Frequency-first Goal: Double monetisation per UDV
The Big Insight: The highest-frequency savings apps (Fetch, Temu, Shopkick) have decoupled "reason to open" from "intent to transact." Groupon currently requires purchase intent to justify an open. Every idea below creates an open-worthy trigger independent of active deal hunting.

Ideas

0 Foundation: Savings Wallet / Groupon Credits Balance
Enabler

Not a standalone feature — an enabler for everything below. A visible, growing balance ("You've earned $14.20 this month") is the retention glue that makes streaks, spins, receipts, and referrals compound. Fetch, Ibotta, and Shopkick all use an accumulating balance as the primary daily-open driver. Users check their balance even when not shopping.

1 Daily Deal Drop + Check-in Streak
POC: Low effort Recommended
What

3-5 curated deals rotate every 24 hours (visible only to app users). Opening the app daily builds a streak — hit 7 days and unlock a bonus (e.g. extra 10% off next purchase, exclusive deal access). Miss a day, lose the streak.

Why it drives frequency

Loss aversion is the strongest behavioural lever. Duolingo users who hit 7-day streaks are 3.6x more likely to stay engaged long-term. Temu's daily check-in drives 89% interaction rates. The time-limited deal rotation creates genuine FOMO — today's deals won't be here tomorrow.

Groupon fit

Local deals already have natural scarcity and freshness. The app install base is the distribution channel. Coupons team could curate the daily selection from existing inventory — no new supply needed.

Risk: Streak rewards must be concrete (credits, exclusive deal unlock) — not abstract. Duolingo streaks work because the daily activity has intrinsic value (learning). Groupon streaks are extrinsically motivated, so the reward needs to carry the weight.
2 "Watch This Deal" — Price & Availability Alerts
POC: Medium effort Recommended
What

Let users save deals/categories they're interested in ("watching"). Notify them when: a deal restarts, a new deal appears in their watched category, or a price drops. Like Honey's Droplist but for local deals.

Why it drives frequency

Honey's Droplist model proves that deferred desire + permission-based alerts has the highest re-engagement quality. Users opt in, so notifications feel like a service, not spam. Each alert is a high-intent return visit.

Groupon fit

Groupon's deals are inherently cyclical — restaurants rerun offers, seasonal deals return. Currently there's no way for a user to say "tell me when there's a spa deal near me under $50." This fills a real gap.

3 Spin-to-Win / Daily Reward Game
POC: Low-Med effort
What

One daily spin (slot machine, scratch card, wheel) that awards variable rewards: Groupon credits, percentage-off coupons, or entries into a monthly prize draw. Variable-ratio reward schedule.

Why it drives frequency

Temu's spin wheels drive 89.4% interaction rates. Fetch Play boosted CLV by 10% and monthly opens by 5%. Over 50% of Temu users made unplanned purchases after gamified interactions. Same mechanism as slot machines — maximises compulsive opens.

Groupon fit

"Entertainment-first, commerce-second." Works if Groupon is willing to give away small credits to drive daily opens. Cost per daily open is tiny vs. paid re-engagement. Works well with price-sensitive audience.

Brand fit question: Temu's stats come from users already in-app for cheap shopping. Groupon's audience is different — spin wheels may feel incongruent. Needs discussion.
4 Community Picks / Social Deal Voting
POC: Medium effort
What

Users upvote deals. A "Trending" or "Community Picks" feed surfaces the highest-voted deals. Optional later phase: users submit external deals (Slickdeals model).

Why it drives frequency

Slickdeals converts 40% of its 12M MAU to DAU. Social proof ("68,000 people liked this deal") makes deals feel discovered, not sold. Voting gives users agency.

Groupon fit

Moderate. No community infrastructure today. Starting with just upvotes on existing deals is low-risk, but upvotes need critical mass to produce meaningful signal.

5 Receipt Scanning → Groupon Credits
POC: High effort
What

Scan any local receipt (restaurant, salon, gym) to earn Groupon credits. Bonus credits for Groupon merchant receipts. Post-purchase touchpoint for every local spend.

Why it drives frequency

Fetch gets more daily opens than Starbucks by making "scan any receipt" a zero-friction habit. Ibotta drives $500M+ revenue. Attaching to existing behaviour is the fastest path to habit formation.

Groupon fit

Groupon could own "local services receipt scanning" — a category Fetch/Ibotta don't cover. Best pursued via partnership, not DIY build.

6 "Groupon Select" Reinvention → Daily Exclusive Access
POC: Low-Med effort
What

Revamp Select ($4.99/mo) from flat discount to daily-engagement membership: exclusive "Member Deal of the Day," early access, streak bonuses, quarterly "Big Savings Report."

Why it drives frequency

Amazon Prime (90%+ renewal) and Costco (90.4% renewal) prove paid membership drives frequency through "fee recovery" psychology. Daily exclusive content gives a reason to open because you're a member.

Groupon fit

Select already exists — infrastructure is there. Gap is perceived value and daily engagement hook. Depends on current Select retention data.

7 Geo-Push: "You're Near a Deal"
POC: Medium effort
What

Contextual push when a user is near a Groupon merchant (within 200m): "The spa next door has 40% off today." Passive, location-triggered engagement.

Why it drives frequency

Shopkick leads all shopping apps in frequency because physical location is the trigger. Notification arrives at the exact moment of opportunity. Proximity-triggered notifications convert 2-3x higher than standard push.

Groupon fit

Very high — Groupon's USP is local. This turns every walk past a merchant into a potential transaction. Strongest natural advantage on this list.

8 Curated Local Content — "What to Do This Weekend"
POC: Low effort Recommended
What

Weekly push / in-app section: "Top 5 things to do in [City] this weekend" — curated from Groupon's deal inventory + editorial recommendations. Think The Strategist but for local experiences.

Why it drives frequency

Creates a weekly ritual. Content-driven engagement is lower friction than transactional — users browse for inspiration. The Strategist earns $2M+/month from this model.

Groupon fit

Natural. Nobody else can say "here are 5 great restaurant deals near you this weekend" with actual attached offers. Positions the app as a planning tool, not just a discount marketplace. Most defensible idea on the list.

9 Last-Minute Local Deals (HotelTonight Model)
POC: Medium effort Recommended
What

Fill merchant dead time — empty restaurant tables Tuesday lunch, salon gaps at 4pm, gym off-peak slots. "3 spa slots left today, 60% off." Deep discounts on genuinely time-sensitive inventory.

Why it drives frequency

Creates urgency-driven opens independent of user intent. Unlike standard deals (available for weeks), these expire in hours. Users learn to check "what's available right now near me?" — a fundamentally different, higher-frequency behaviour.

Groupon fit

Uniquely strong. Nobody else can surface "your favourite salon has a 4pm gap today, 50% off." Intersection of local advantage + time-sensitivity + real merchant value.

10 Merchant Self-Service — Instant Deal Publishing
POC: High effort
What

Merchants publish deals in 2-3 clicks. Real-time "flash deals" activated when business is slow ("Tuesday afternoon, 50% off — 2 hours only").

Why it drives frequency

Flash deals create urgency and unpredictability. Users check back because "something new might have dropped." Merchant-generated supply scales inventory without Groupon's sales team.

Groupon fit

Addresses supply scaling and frequency. High effort for Core (platform changes). Could test manually first — merchants message a rep, deal goes live within an hour.

Recommendation: 4-Concept Bundle

These aren't competing — they're complementary engagement layers targeting different user behaviours.

Primary: Daily Deal Drop + Streak (#1) Daily habit engine. Strongest behavioural evidence, lowest effort, uses existing inventory. Pair with Savings Wallet so credits accumulate visibly.
Complementary: Watch This Deal (#2) Passive re-engagement for users who won't check daily but will return when something matches. Captures the intent-based segment that streaks miss.
Sleeper: Weekend Content (#8) Easiest to ship, most defensible. Positions Groupon as a planning tool, not just a discount marketplace.
Uniquely Groupon: Last-Minute Deals (#9) Nobody else can do "your salon has a 4pm gap." Intersection of local advantage + time-sensitivity + real merchant value.

Risks to Name Upfront

  • Cannibalisation: Could daily deals train users to wait instead of buying now? Mitigate by making daily deals additive inventory (flash/exclusive), not discounts on what's already available.
  • Push notification fatigue: 4-5 ideas rely on push. Need a notification strategy — one daily digest ("Morning Brief"), not 5 separate pushes.
  • App landing experience: Current app is built for purchase intent. Opens without intent need a "non-transactional" landing zone (daily deals tab, trending section).

What We Considered and Set Aside

  • 3P Marketplace (goods replacement): Revenue play, not frequency. Being handled separately by Core.
  • Full cashback programme: Not differentiated — every competitor has one. Promo test on Coupons pages is the separate workstream.
  • Social feed / UGC: Requires community flywheel Groupon doesn't have. Cold-start problem is severe.
  • Referral/invite loop: Good growth mechanic, but complements rather than drives frequency. Layer it on later.

Alternative Monetisation — Brainstorm Ideas

v2 — now includes affiliate coupons as an engagement lever
For discussion: Martin + Becky, 2026-03-26 Lens: Frequency-first Goal: Double monetisation per UDV
Big Insight #1: The highest-frequency savings apps (Fetch, Temu, Shopkick) have decoupled "reason to open" from "intent to transact." Groupon currently requires purchase intent to justify an open. Every idea below creates an open-worthy trigger independent of active deal hunting.
Big Insight #2: Groupon's affiliate coupons content is a hidden frequency asset stuck on the web. People need promo codes constantly — every online purchase is a coupon moment. This content currently lives on groupon.com/coupons (SEO-driven, visit-and-leave). Moving it into the app creates a "save everywhere" utility layer — the bridge between "I open Groupon for a spa deal once a month" and "I open Groupon every time I shop online." Becky's quote nails it: "How do we use our affiliate content to drive people opening the app more?"

The Cross-Pollination Flywheel

Affiliate coupons and core deals reinforce each other when both live in the app.

User needs a Nike code → Opens Groupon app → Sees daily deal / local offer → Books a spa deal → Earns credits in wallet → Comes back for next code

Every coupon lookup is an exposure to a core deal. Every core deal visit is an exposure to coupons. The wallet connects both.

Part A: Core App Engagement Ideas

Ideas that drive app frequency through local deals, gamification, and content — independent of affiliate coupons.

0 Foundation: Savings Wallet / Groupon Credits
Enabler Core + Affiliate

The connective tissue for everything. A visible, growing balance ("You've earned $14.20 this month") that accumulates from streaks, coupon use, receipts, and referrals. Users check their balance even when not shopping. This is what makes the flywheel spin — credits earned from affiliate coupons can be spent on core deals and vice versa.

1 Daily Deal Drop + Check-in Streak
POC: Low effort Recommended Core deals
What

3-5 curated deals rotate every 24 hours (app-only). Opening daily builds a streak — hit 7 days, unlock a bonus. Miss a day, lose the streak.

Why it drives frequency

Loss aversion is the strongest behavioural lever. Duolingo 7-day streaks → 3.6x long-term retention. Temu daily check-in → 89% interaction rate. Time-limited rotation creates genuine FOMO.

Groupon fit

Local deals already have natural scarcity. Coupons team curates from existing inventory — no new supply needed.

Affiliate enhancement: Mix in top online coupon codes alongside local deals in the daily rotation. "Today: 40% off spa near you + 25% off Nike + new Sephora code." Now the daily drop is relevant whether you're shopping local or online.
Risk: Streak rewards must be concrete (credits, exclusive unlock) — Groupon streaks are extrinsically motivated, so the reward carries the weight.
2 "Watch This Deal" — Price & Availability Alerts
POC: Medium effort Recommended Core + Affiliate
What

Save deals, categories, or retailers you're interested in. Get notified when: a local deal restarts, a new code appears for a watched store, or a discount improves. Honey's Droplist meets Groupon's local deals.

Why it drives frequency

Deferred desire + permission-based alerts = highest-quality re-engagement. Users opt in, so notifications feel like a service. Each alert is a high-intent return visit.

Groupon fit

Local deals are cyclical (restaurants rerun offers). Online codes appear and expire constantly. No user can currently say "tell me when there's a spa deal under $50 near me" or "alert me when Nike has 20%+ off." Both are natural.

3 Spin-to-Win / Daily Reward Game
POC: Low-Med effort Core deals
What

Daily spin awarding variable rewards: Groupon credits, coupons, prize draw entries. Variable-ratio schedule (slot machine mechanic).

Why it drives frequency

Temu spin wheels → 89% interaction. Fetch Play → +10% CLV, +5% monthly opens. Over 50% of Temu users made unplanned purchases after gamified interactions.

Brand fit question: May feel incongruent with Groupon's brand. Needs discussion.
4 Community Picks / Social Deal Voting
POC: Medium effort Core + Affiliate
What

Upvote local deals and online codes. "Trending" feed surfaces the most popular. The "trending near you" angle combines community signal with local relevance.

Why it drives frequency

Slickdeals converts 40% MAU → DAU through community curation. Social proof makes deals feel discovered, not sold.

Affiliate angle: Coupon codes are perfect for community voting — "this code actually works" is incredibly valuable signal. Code verification via user votes solves a real trust problem.
5 Receipt Scanning → Groupon Credits
POC: High effort Core deals
What

Scan any local receipt (restaurant, salon, gym) → earn credits. Bonus for Groupon merchant receipts. Best via partnership (Fetch/Ibotta), not DIY.

Why it drives frequency

Fetch gets more daily opens than Starbucks. Attaching to existing behaviour (getting a receipt) = fastest habit formation.

6 "Groupon Select" Reinvention → Daily Exclusive Access
POC: Low-Med effort Core + Affiliate
What

Revamp Select ($4.99/mo): exclusive "Member Deal of the Day," early access, streak bonuses, quarterly savings report. Members also get exclusive online coupon codes not available to non-members.

Why it drives frequency

Amazon Prime 90%+ renewal, Costco 90.4% — paid membership drives frequency through "fee recovery" psychology. Daily exclusive content = reason to open as a member.

7 Geo-Push: "You're Near a Deal"
POC: Medium effort Core deals
What

Contextual push near a Groupon merchant: "The spa next door has 40% off today." Proximity-triggered notifications convert 2-3x higher than standard push.

Groupon fit

Groupon's strongest natural advantage — local. Turns every walk past a merchant into a potential transaction.

8 Curated Local Content — "What to Do This Weekend"
POC: Low effort Recommended Core deals
What

Weekly push / in-app section: "Top 5 things to do in [City] this weekend" — curated from deal inventory + editorial picks.

Why it drives frequency

Creates a weekly ritual. Content-driven engagement is lower friction than transactional. The Strategist earns $2M+/month. Nobody else can say "here are 5 restaurant deals near you this weekend" with attached offers.

9 Last-Minute Local Deals (HotelTonight Model)
POC: Medium effort Recommended Core deals
What

Fill merchant dead time — empty tables Tuesday lunch, salon gaps at 4pm. "3 spa slots left today, 60% off." Expires in hours, not weeks.

Why it drives frequency

Users learn to check "what's available right now near me?" — fundamentally different, higher-frequency behaviour. Nobody else can surface "your salon has a 4pm gap today."

10 Merchant Self-Service — Instant Deal Publishing
POC: High effort Core deals
What

Merchants publish flash deals in 2-3 clicks when business is slow. Creates urgency and unpredictability. Could test manually first.

Part B: Affiliate Coupons as an Engagement Layer

Ideas that use Groupon's existing coupons/promo code content to drive app frequency. These complement Part A — they're not separate initiatives.

A1 Follow Stores + Code Alerts
POC: Low effort Recommended Affiliate coupons
What

Let users follow 10-20 favourite online retailers (Nike, Sephora, Amazon, etc.) in the app. Push a notification when a new code appears or an existing code improves for a followed store. "Nike just dropped a 25% off code — tap to grab it."

Why it drives frequency

People shop at the same stores repeatedly. Honey's store-follow alerts and Droplist drive the majority of their re-engagement. Each notification is permission-based (user chose the store) so it feels like a service, not spam. And every alert-driven app open exposes the user to local deals too.

Groupon fit

Groupon has codes for thousands of retailers already. The content exists — it's just not personalized or push-enabled. This is the single lowest-effort, highest-impact move for affiliate content in the app.

POC

Follow button on retailer pages + notification trigger when new code is detected + simple "My Stores" screen in-app. 4-week test with a user cohort.

A2 Weekly "Best Codes" Digest
POC: Low effort Recommended Affiliate coupons
What

Every Monday morning, push a single notification: "This week's top codes: 30% off ASOS, 25% Nike, 20% Uber Eats + 3 more." Links to a curated in-app landing page. Personalised by shopping history / followed stores over time.

Why it drives frequency

Predictable cadence = habit formation. This is Flipp's playbook (weekly grocery flyers → 10M+ downloads, 4.8 stars). Users learn "check Groupon Monday for the week's best codes." The digest format also solves push fatigue — one notification, many codes.

Groupon fit

The content already exists — this is purely curation + push + a landing page. Could be manual initially (editor picks 7 codes), automated later. Pairs naturally with the Weekend Content idea (#8) — Monday codes + Friday local picks = two weekly rituals.

A3 Code Expiry Countdown + "Use It or Lose It" Alerts
POC: Low effort Affiliate coupons
What

When a user saves or views a code, track it. If the code is expiring within 24 hours, push an alert: "Your 30% off ASOS code expires tonight." Prominent expiry timers on saved codes in-app.

Why it drives frequency

Urgency + loss aversion. A code you saved that's about to expire is nearly impossible to ignore. This is the same FOMO mechanic as flash sales, but applied to something the user already expressed interest in. High-intent, high-conversion notifications.

Groupon fit

Codes already have expiry dates in Groupon's system. This is mostly a notification trigger + UI treatment. Very low effort, very high engagement potential.

A4 Web-to-App Bridge: "Save This Code to the App"
POC: Low-Med effort Affiliate coupons
What

On groupon.com/coupons pages (SEO traffic), add a CTA: "Save this code to the Groupon app" that deep-links into the app with the code pre-loaded. Converts one-time web visitors into app users. Once in the app, they're in the ecosystem.

Why it drives frequency

Groupon's coupons pages get millions of SEO visits. Currently these users arrive, grab a code, and leave. This is the bridge — convert web traffic into app installs/opens. Each converted user enters the flywheel (sees local deals, earns credits, gets push alerts).

Groupon fit

Uses existing traffic that Groupon already owns. No new content creation. The deep-link technology exists (branch.io, etc.). This is the funnel that feeds all other engagement ideas.

A5 Post-Use Credits: "Did This Code Work?"
POC: Medium effort Affiliate coupons
What

After a user copies a code, prompt them to confirm: "Did this code work? Yes → earn 10 credits. No → we'll find you another." This creates a closing loop + code verification data + credits flowing into the wallet.

Why it drives frequency

Turns a one-way interaction (grab code, leave) into a round-trip (grab code, come back, confirm, earn credits). The credit reward makes the return visit worthwhile. The verification data improves code quality for everyone — solving the #1 frustration with coupon sites (broken codes).

Groupon fit

Solves a real product problem (code quality) while driving engagement. The credits feed the Savings Wallet. Could also power the Community Picks idea (#4) — "verified by 847 users" is powerful social proof.

A6 "Save Everywhere" — Unified Savings Dashboard
POC: Medium effort Core + Affiliate
What

A single screen showing all your savings opportunities in one place: local deals near you + online codes for your favourite stores + available credits + active watches. "Your personalised savings hub." Think Rakuten dashboard but combining local + online.

Why it drives frequency

Positions Groupon as "the one app for all savings" — not just local deals, not just coupons. The combination is the moat: nobody else has both local deals AND online codes in the same app. Users open to see "what can I save on today?" across all categories.

Groupon fit

Groupon literally has both content types — local deals (core) and online codes (affiliate). They just live in separate silos today. Merging them into a unified experience is Groupon's unique positioning that Honey, Rakuten, or RetailMeNot can't replicate.

Recommendation: The "Save Everywhere" Bundle

Six complementary layers that turn Groupon from "deal marketplace I visit monthly" into "savings companion I open daily." The affiliate ideas are the frequency engine; the core ideas are the monetisation engine.

1. Daily Deal Drop + Streak (#1) — daily habit engine Mix local deals + top online codes in one daily rotation. Streak rewards feed the Savings Wallet.
2. Follow Stores + Code Alerts (A1) — the everyday utility Users follow their favourite online stores. Alerts drive app opens for every shopping moment, not just local deals. This is the bridge between monthly and daily engagement.
3. Watch This Deal (#2) — passive re-engagement Captures intent-based users who won't check daily but will return when something matches. Works for both local deals and online stores.
4. Weekend Content (#8) — weekly planning ritual "Top 5 things to do this weekend." Positions Groupon as a planning tool. Easiest to ship, most defensible.
5. Last-Minute Deals (#9) — uniquely Groupon Nobody else can do "your salon has a 4pm gap." Highest differentiation on the list.
6. Weekly Best Codes Digest (A2) — Monday morning ritual Pairs with Weekend Content for a two-beat weekly rhythm: Monday codes + Friday local picks.

Foundation: Savings Wallet (#0) connects everything. Credits earned from code alerts, streaks, and post-use verification are spent on core local deals. This is the flywheel.

Risks to Name Upfront

  • Cannibalisation: Could daily deals train users to wait? Mitigate: daily deals are additive inventory (flash/exclusive), not discounts on existing.
  • Push notification fatigue: Multiple ideas rely on push. Solution: one "Morning Brief" digest aggregating all signals, not 5 separate pushes. Store alerts can fire individually (permission-based).
  • App landing experience: Current app is built for purchase intent. Non-intent opens need a "savings hub" landing zone — the Unified Dashboard (A6) solves this long-term.
  • Affiliate attribution integrity: Honey's cookie-overwriting scandal is a cautionary tale. Groupon must use clean, transparent attribution. Never overwrite another affiliate's cookies.
  • Coupons content quality: Broken codes are the #1 frustration. Post-Use Credits (A5) and Community Voting (#4) help, but code quality needs to be high enough to earn trust.

What We Considered and Set Aside

  • 3P Marketplace (goods replacement): Revenue play, not frequency. Being handled separately by Core.
  • Full cashback programme: Not differentiated. Promo test on Coupons pages is the separate workstream.
  • Social feed / UGC: Cold-start problem too severe for Groupon today.
  • Referral/invite loop: Growth mechanic, not frequency driver. Layer on later.
  • Browser extension (Genie-for-core): Already exists as Genie — separate initiative with separate install base. Focus here is on the core app.
